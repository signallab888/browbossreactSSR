  SECTION 8 — FAQ

  Does a lash lift damage your natural lashes?
  No — as long as it's done correctly. The two main causes of damage are over-processing the lash (leaving solution on too long) and going back too
  soon. YUMI's keratin formula is specifically designed to nourish while it lifts, which is the opposite of damaging. Our technicians are certified and 
  trained to time every step properly.
                                                                                                                                                        
  How long do results actually last?                                                                                                                    
  Most clients see a full lift for 8–10 weeks. Some go up to 12. The lift doesn't "fall out" — it grows out naturally with your lash cycle, so it fades
  gradually rather than all at once.                                                                                                                    
                  
  Can I wear mascara after?                                                                                                                             
  Yes — wait 24 hours, then water-based mascara is completely fine. Most clients find they don't need it. If you want the darkest result possible,
  adding a tint to your appointment is the better option.                                                                                               
   
  What's the difference between a lash lift and extensions?                                                                                             
  Extensions add synthetic fibers to your natural lashes using adhesive. They require fills every 2–3 weeks to stay looking full, and if not maintained,
   the grow-out can look patchy. A lash lift works entirely with your own lashes — no glue, no fibers, no fills. You book it once and come back when    
  your natural lash cycle is done.
                                                                                                                                                        
  I have short lashes. Will it still work?
  It depends on the actual length. Very short lashes may not give you a dramatic result, but we can assess this before we start — we won't go through
  with it if it's not going to do anything for you. Most clients with short but present lashes see a real difference.                                   
   
  How soon can I book again?                                                                                                                            
  Wait at least 8 weeks. This gives the previous lift time to grow out and keeps your lashes from being over-processed. If you feel like the curl is
  gone before 8 weeks, it's usually because your lash growth cycle is faster — not a reason to go sooner.                                               
   
  What do I need to do before the appointment?                                                                                                          
  Come with clean lashes. No mascara, no eyelash curler in the 24 hours before. Remove contact lenses before we start. That's it.
                                                                                                                                                        
  Is it safe during pregnancy?
  The treatment is non-invasive and considered safe during pregnancy. If you're in your first trimester or have any concerns, check with your doctor    
  first. We're happy to answer any specific questions when you call.                                                                                    
   
  What if I don't like how it looks?                                                                                                                    
  The honest answer: the lift cannot be reversed. If you're nervous about going too dramatic, tell us before we start. We'll choose a subtle rod and a
  natural-looking angle — we'd rather undershoot than overdo it. The goal is always to enhance what's already there.
